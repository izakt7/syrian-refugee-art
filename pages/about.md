## Syrian Refugee Art Exhibition

This digital exhibition showcases works by artists who have documented and expressed the experiences of displacement, resilience, and hope in the context of the Syrian refugee crisis. Through paintings, photographs, installations, and multimedia works, these artists transform personal and collective suffering into art that demands our attention, empathy, and action.

### Our Mission

The Syrian conflict, which began in 2011, has led to one of the largest humanitarian crises of our time, forcing millions to flee their homes. This exhibition serves to:

- **Preserve Stories:** Document the experiences of Syrian refugees through artistic expression
- **Foster Empathy:** Help visitors understand the human impact of displacement
- **Inspire Action:** Connect viewers with ways to support refugees and advocate for change
- **Build Understanding:** Bridge the gap between refugees and host communities

### The Collection

This collection features diverse artistic responses to the Syrian refugee crisis, including:

- **Paintings and Prints:** Visual narratives of displacement and hope
- **Photographs:** Documentary evidence of refugee experiences
- **Multimedia Works:** Videos and audio pieces that capture personal stories
- **Installations:** Works that contextualize the crisis within physical spaces

Each artwork tells a unique story while contributing to a larger narrative about resilience, loss, and the enduring human spirit.

### How to Use This Site

- **Browse the Collection:** Explore artworks by medium, location, or artist
- **Compare Artworks:** Use our comparison tool to explore connections between pieces
- **View Statistics:** Understand the scale of the crisis through data visualizations
- **Explore Geography:** See where artworks are from and understand migration patterns
- **Take Action:** Find ways to donate, volunteer, or advocate for refugees

### About the Platform

This exhibition is built using [Wax](https://minicomp.github.io/wiki/wax/), a minimal computing framework for creating digital exhibitions. Wax is designed to be lightweight, accessible, and sustainable, making it ideal for preserving and sharing cultural heritage.
