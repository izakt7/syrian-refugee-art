## Fact Check: Syrian Refugee Crisis

Share these facts to help combat misinformation and promote understanding:

### Key Facts

**Scale of the Crisis**
- Over **13 million Syrians** have been displaced since 2011
- **6.8 million** are internally displaced within Syria
- **6.6 million** are refugees in neighboring countries and beyond
- This represents **more than half** of Syria's pre-war population

**Where Refugees Are**
- **Turkey**: 3.6 million (largest host country)
- **Lebanon**: 1.5 million (highest per capita)
- **Jordan**: 1.3 million
- **Iraq**: 250,000
- **Egypt**: 140,000
- **Germany**: 800,000+ (largest in Europe)
- **Other countries**: Hundreds of thousands across Europe, North America, and beyond

**Who They Are**
- **50% are children** under 18 years old
- Many have been displaced for over a decade
- Most refugees live in urban areas, not camps
- Many are highly educated professionals

**Current Situation**
- The conflict continues, making return unsafe for most
- Refugee populations face economic hardship and limited opportunities
- Education access remains a critical challenge
- Many countries have reduced resettlement programs

### Common Misconceptions

❌ **Myth**: Most refugees are in Europe  
✅ **Fact**: 85% of Syrian refugees are in neighboring Middle Eastern countries

❌ **Myth**: Refugees are a security threat  
✅ **Fact**: Refugees undergo extensive security screening; they are fleeing violence, not causing it

❌ **Myth**: Refugees don't want to work  
✅ **Fact**: Most refugees want to work but face legal barriers and limited opportunities

❌ **Myth**: Refugees receive generous benefits  
✅ **Fact**: Most refugees live in poverty with limited access to services
